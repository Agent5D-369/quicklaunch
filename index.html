Answered Honestly

Each phase can be scoped independently - but the path is designed as a deliberate sequence for good reason. If you're a warm referral or have already completed equivalent clarity work with another framework, we'll assess exactly where you're starting from on the fit call. Skipping phases that haven't been genuinely completed is how most projects end up right back at square one. We'll be honest with you about what you actually need.

Typical: 2–6 weeks. Fast track: 10–14 days. Extended: 8–12 weeks for larger stakeholder groups or complex organizations. The timeline is driven by your decision gates - not by a calendar slot we need to fill. You move forward when the gate criteria are genuinely met, not before.

Phase 1 works best with the people who actually make or block decisions - not everyone, but the right ones. We've run the Purpose Workshop with groups from 3 to 30 people. Phases 2 and 3 can be founder-led or team-led depending on your situation. We'll tell you exactly who needs to be in the room before we start.

It's none of those categories and all three at once. The most accurate frame is architecture work. You won't leave with vague recommendations or an inspiring PDF - you'll leave with specific, documented deliverables that your team can operate from immediately. Think of it as installing infrastructure, not giving advice.

That is exactly who this path is designed for. Phase 1 is specifically built to work in environments with active conflict, misalignment, or power confusion. You do not need to have things "sorted out" before you start - sorting things out is what the path does. The only requirement is that the right people are willing to show up and engage honestly.

A consultant tells you what to do. QuickLaunch installs the structure for you to govern yourself. The deliverables belong to you. The operating logic lives inside your team - not in our heads. This is deliberate: we are building your structural capacity, not your dependency on us. When the path is complete, you should need us less - not more.
