The Logic

Why the Sequence Matters

Why Phase 1 must come first:

Most projects fail not because the idea is wrong but because the purpose is vague. Without a decision-grade purpose, every downstream decision is contested. You can't validate an offer you haven't clearly defined. You can't build a team structure around a mission nobody agrees on.

Why Phase 2 must come before building:

The most expensive mistake in any project is building before proving. Phase 2 proves demand with actual market evidence - not surveys, not assumptions, not enthusiasm. Real conversations. Real presale signals. Real go/no-go decisions backed by data.

Why Phase 3 determines whether anything gets delivered:

Most projects have a purpose and a validated idea but no operating container to execute from. Phase 3 installs the structure - roles, decision rights, and phased plans - that allows the validated MVP to be built and delivered without friction, confusion, or founder bottleneck.

🟢 Decision Gate A: Purpose Lock

Move forward to Phase 2 only when all four criteria are met:

  • Purpose statement is decision-grade: specific Dream Target, real pain, clear transformation
  • A single primary 2am problem is chosen - not three, not a category
  • Non-goals and scope guardrails are written and accepted
  • Stakeholder alignment is achieved, or disagreements are clearly documented with resolution owners

If gate is not met: A 60-minute Purpose Refinement Mini-Sprint is scheduled before Phase 2 begins. No extra charge.

The 3-Session Sequence

S1

Session 1: Market Ignition & Hooks

90 minutes

Build the targeting, messaging, and outreach system to generate real market signal.

Micro-pockets map Top 3 hooks 10 awakening posts Outreach scripts Outreach tracker

Evidence Window 1 (3–7 Days Between Sessions)

Your job: 25 ethical outreaches, 5–10 real conversations, 1 partner invitation attempt, collection of real objection and language data. This is where most programs skip the uncomfortable work. We don't.

Decision Gate B: Signal Check

  • Real replies and conversations are happening with the right people
  • The pain language is resonating - people are nodding, not politely listening
  • At least a few people ask "how do I do this?" or "what's next?"

If gate not met: Revise hooks and target pockets. Rerun the evidence window. Do not proceed until signal is real.

S2

Session 2: Presale Validation

90 minutes

Build the validation infrastructure to convert interest into actual commitments.

Promise page draft Micro-webinar outline Fit call script Presale offer sheet Proof thresholds Stop rules

Presale Window (7–14 Days Between Sessions)

Your job: run 1 micro-webinar, conduct 5–12 fit calls, pursue presale commitments or deposits, invite MVP Council prospects. This is where you find out if people will actually pay - before you build anything.

🟢 Decision Gate C: Presale Threshold

  • Presale commitments hit the agreed threshold (defined in Session 2)
  • Or clear demand signals show a simple pivot will convert

If gate not met: Pause the build. Adjust the offer or pivot to the first-win version. We do not build on unproven ground.

S3

Session 3: MVP Council & Blueprint Forge

90 minutes

Translate validated demand into a fully scoped, buildable roadmap.

MVP Council plan QuickLaunch MVP Blueprint Build sprint backlog Onboarding plan Phase 3 handoff pack

Add-On

Market Expansion
Engine™

The bridge into retainers, long-term builds, and platform plays.

This phase is not required to complete the core path - and it should not be started before execution is genuinely stable. This is not scaling chaos. It is scaling systems that already work. Available only when Decision Gate E criteria are met.

Decision Gate E: Growth Readiness

  • Delivery is stable - the MVP is running and delivering the promised transformation
  • Onboarding works - new participants/clients move through it without confusion
  • Early proof exists - real testimonials, results, case studies
  • The team has capacity to run and sustain a growth cadence
